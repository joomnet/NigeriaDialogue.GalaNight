A Gala Night To Remember

   

    


To kick off 2014, Nigeria Dialogue presents its Black-Tie Gala Night, to be held at the prestigious Plaisterers Hall, One London Wall, EC2Y 5JU, the event will pave the way to support and raise awareness for Nigeria Dialogue projects throughout 2014 and beyond, in our effort to change the world and contribute to our nation by doing what we can do for the continuous growth of our country.


We invite you to join this revolution on Saturday 18th January 2013 from 5pm for a memorable evening solely dedicated to raising awareness & fundraising for a good cause. The evening will begin with a Media Red carpet, followed by a hosted Champagne and Cocktail Reception with the opportunity to mingle and network with CEOs of multi-million pound organisation, a diverse mix of professionals from all industries and career levels, and celebrities from the movie, sports, fashion, & entertainment industries.

A formal dinner and the start of the program will follow the drinks reception at 6:30pm. One of London’s finest MC and comedian, Wale Gates will serve as emcee. We are expecting over 200 people to attend so do not miss out on this exciting evening. Throughout the evening guests will be treated to an array of thrilling experiences.

Live performances from The Outlander Boys with a melody of Jazz and taking us back to the old school, Spoken word & performance poetry at its finest, Notable Guest Speakers/policy makers from Nigeria, various premiere videos on Nigeria Dialogue activities and from our serviced charities. Our prestigious DJ will entertain us and deliver the ultimate experience to rock the night away.

Nigeria Dialogue intends to lead a nationwide campaign in 2014 to enlighten the Nigerian public about good governance and frame the key issues facing the nation before the general elections.

We plan to work with USAID, Amnesty International and other NGO’s in monitoring the 2015 elections to ensure a fair election.
